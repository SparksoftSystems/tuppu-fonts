Ramsina is a revival of early 20th‑century East Syriac metal type. The original design, created by Esho Marcus and known as East Syriac Marcus, updated with modern OpenType features by Sargon Hasso and developed in collaboration with the SIL Writing Systems Technology group.

Idiqlat is a redesigned East Syriac typeface (its name means “Tigris River”), optimized for web and mobile and available in ExtraLight, Light, and Regular weights. Both fonts support full East Syriac Unicode and advanced OpenType stylistic sets and alternates.

For more information, please visit the Modern Assyrian Fonts for Digital and Print Use.

Important:

If you’re installing fonts on your computer or using them in print/desktop applications, download the TTF files. If you plan to embed these fonts on web pages, download the WOFF and WOFF2 files — they are compressed and optimized for web use.
